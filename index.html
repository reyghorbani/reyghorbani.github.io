B.S. Thesis Project

Adsorption Processes Laboratory, University of Tehran

Encapsulating milk-derived Micro-RNAs in lipid nanoparticles.

The purpose of this project is to identify the behavior of encapsulated lipid nanoparticles while presented in the human body. This study is now conducted at the Adsorption Processes Laboratory, University of Tehran, Tehran, Iran. Many studies have indicated that liposomes can be a proper candidate for encapsulation and delivery of drugs. In this study, the preparation and application of liposomes for encapsulation of milk-derived Micro-RNAs are being studied and discussed. Particularly, we are focusing on the application of milk-derived Micro-RNAs for a new role in cancer treatments. Furthermore, we are realizing PEGylation as a strategy for improving nanoparticle-based drug delivery, in order to enhance the possibility of proper delivery for nanoparticles in the body. Based on the generated information, currently, we are looking for the best way to extract Micro-RNAs from human or cow milk to encapsulate the extracted microRNAs in the prepared liposomes as a novel method for cancer treatments.

Research Assistant

Adsorption Processes Laboratory, University of Tehran

Applications of Indirect Biosensors in the Detection of Lung Cancer Using Respiratory Exhaust Gases.

The purpose of the study was to evaluate the Biosensorsâ€™ role in the early diagnosis of Lung cancer. This study was conducted at the Adsorption Processes Laboratory, University of Tehran, Tehran, Iran. Nowadays, the application of biosensors has flourished in various industries, which play a decisive role in the future of new technologies. Many studied, conducted all over the world, have demonstrated the crucial role of novel bio-sensors in cancer diagnosis, cancer treatments, the food industry, and the elimination of environmental pollution. Particularly, in this study, we were focusing on the role of new bio-sensors in the detection of lung cancer by using respiratory exhausted gases. In this study, patients with lung disease were asked to blow in a container to analyze their breath and use a designed bio-sensor for diagnosing lung cancer. In addition, we examined another group of patients, who suffered lung disease, to establish the role of designed bio-sensor for early diagnosis too.
